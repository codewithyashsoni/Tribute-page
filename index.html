Albert Einstein

The Genius Who Redefined Science

Albert Einstein, the brilliant physicist, revolutionized science with his theory of relativity and reshaped our understanding of the universe.

Here's a time line of Albert Einstein's life:

  • 1879: Albert Einstein is born on March 14 in Ulm, Germany.
  • 1884: At age 5, Einstein is introduced to a compass, sparking his interest in science.
  • 1895: Fails the entrance exam to the Swiss Federal Polytechnic in Zurich but passes on his second attempt.
  • 1900: Graduates from the Polytechnic Institute with a teaching degree in mathematics and physics.
  • 1905: Publishes four groundbreaking papers in the Annals of Physics, including the Theory of Special Relativity and the equation E=mc². This year becomes known as his "Miracle Year" (Annus Mirabilis).
  • 1915: Develops the General Theory of Relativity, redefining gravity and space-time.
  • 1921: Awarded the Nobel Prize in Physics for his explanation of the photoelectric effect.
  • 1933: Leaves Germany due to the rise of the Nazi regime and moves to the United States. Becomes a professor at Princeton University.
  • 1939: Signs a letter to President Roosevelt urging the U.S. to develop atomic research, leading to the Manhattan Project.
  • 1945: Advocates for global peace following the atomic bombings of Hiroshima and Nagasaki.
  • 1952: Declines an offer to become President of Israel.
  • 1955: Albert Einstein dies on April 18 in Princeton, New Jersey, leaving behind a profound scientific legacy.
